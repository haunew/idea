# Academic Paper Writing Guide (English)

## Standard Paper Structure (IMRaD)

### 1. Title
- Be concise, specific, and informative
- Avoid abbreviations and jargon
- 10-15 words recommended
- Use keywords for searchability

### 2. Abstract (200-300 words)
Structure:
- **Background**: 1-2 sentences on the research context
- **Methods**: 1-2 sentences on approach
- **Results**: 2-3 sentences on key findings
- **Conclusion**: 1-2 sentences on implications

Tips:
- Write after completing the paper
- Use past tense for methods/results
- No citations in abstract
- Follow journal's structured/unstructured format

### 3. Keywords
- 3-6 keywords
- Include field-specific terms
- Consider what readers would search for

### 4. Introduction (Funnel Structure)
1. **Broad context** → General problem area
2. **Literature review** → Current state of research
3. **Research gap** → What's missing
4. **Research question** → Your specific aim
5. **Significance** → Why it matters

Tips:
- Move from general to specific
- Cite key literature
- State hypotheses clearly
- Use present tense for established knowledge

### 5. Methods
Include sufficient detail for replication:
- Study design
- Participants/materials
- Data collection procedures
- Analysis methods (software version, statistical tests)
- Ethics approval (if applicable)

Tips:
- Use past tense
- Be precise with numbers
- Reference established methods
- Include supplementary materials if lengthy

### 6. Results
Present findings objectively:
- Use past tense
- Report statistics with effect sizes and confidence intervals
- Use figures and tables effectively
- Don't interpret here—just report

Order:
1. Descriptive statistics
2. Primary outcomes
3. Secondary outcomes
4. Additional analyses

### 7. Discussion
Structure:
1. **Summary** of key findings (1 paragraph)
2. **Interpretation** - what do results mean?
3. **Comparison** with existing literature
4. **Implications** - theoretical and practical
5. **Limitations** - be honest and specific
6. **Future directions** - next steps
7. **Conclusion** - main takeaways

Tips:
- Use present tense for interpretations
- Address all results from previous section
- Don't overstate conclusions
- Connect back to introduction

### 8. References
- Follow journal's specific guidelines
- Use reference manager (Zotero, Mendeley, EndNote)
- Ensure all in-text citations match reference list
- Check DOIs and URLs

## Language Tips

### Academic Writing Style
- **Formal tone**: Avoid contractions, colloquialisms
- **Objective**: Minimize personal opinions; use evidence
- **Precise**: Use specific terms, avoid vague words
- **Concise**: Eliminate redundancy

### Common Phrases

**Introduction:**
- "Recent studies have shown..."
- "However, little is known about..."
- "The purpose of this study is to..."
- "We hypothesized that..."

**Methods:**
- "Participants were recruited from..."
- "Data were collected using..."
- "We conducted [analysis type] to..."

**Results:**
- "The results showed that..."
- "There was a significant [effect/difference]..."
- "Figure 1 illustrates..."

**Discussion:**
- "These findings suggest that..."
- "This is consistent with..."
- "One possible explanation is..."
- "A limitation of this study is..."

## Journal Selection Criteria

Consider:
1. **Scope** - Does your topic fit?
2. **Audience** - Who reads this journal?
3. **Impact Factor** - Reputation in field
4. **Publication type** - Original research, brief report, review?
5. **Open access** - Availability and fees
6. **Timeline** - Review and publication speed

## Revision Checklist

### Content
- [ ] Clear research question/hypothesis
- [ ] Methods fully described
- [ ] Results match methods
- [ ] Discussion addresses all results
- [ ] Conclusions supported by data

### Structure
- [ ] Logical flow between sections
- [ ] Appropriate paragraph length
- [ ] Clear topic sentences
- [ ] Effective transitions

### Language
- [ ] Grammar and spelling checked
- [ ] Consistent terminology
- [ ] Appropriate academic tone
- [ ] No redundant phrases

### Formatting
- [ ] Follows journal guidelines
- [ ] Correct citation format
- [ ] Figures/tables properly numbered
- [ ] Supplementary materials prepared
